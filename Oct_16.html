1. Monday:
Anime Screening in Huxley 130 @ 18:30

Theme: The best of the best

This week we are giving you three Psychological Thrillers. I watched and enjoyed the first two for the most part, so come check it out if they seem interesting.

  • Death Parade. After death, either Heaven or Hell awaits most humans. But for a select few, death brings them to Quindecim—a bar where only pairs of people who die at the same time can enter. An enigmatic figure known as Decim acts as the arbiter, passing judgment on those who wind up at Quindecim by challenging them to a life-threatening game. These games determine if the patron's soul will reincarnate into a new life, or be sent into the void, never to be seen again..
  • Erased. When tragedy is about to strike, Satoru Fujinuma finds himself sent back several minutes before the accident occurs. The detached, 29-year-old manga artist has taken advantage of this powerful yet mysterious phenomenon, which he calls "Revival," to save many lives. However, when he is wrongfully accused of murdering someone close to him, Satoru is sent back to the past once again, but this time to 1988, 18 years in the past. ERASED follows Satoru in his mission to uncover what truly transpired 18 years ago and prevent the death of his classmate while protecting those he cares about in the present
  • The Tatami Galaxy. One autumn evening at a mysterious ramen stand behind the Shimogamo Shrine, a lonely third-year college student bumps into a man with an eggplant-shaped head who calls himself a god of matrimony. Meeting this man causes the student to reflect upon his past two years at college—two years bitterly spent trying to break up couples on campus with his only friend Ozu, a ghoulish-looking man seemingly set on making his life as miserable as possible. Resolving to make the most out of the rest of his college life, the student attempts to ask out the unsociable but kind-hearted underclassman Akashi, yet fails to follow through, prompting him to regret not living out his college life differently. As soon as this thought passes through his head, however, he is hurtled through time and space to the beginning of his years at college and given another chance to live his life.

3. Friday:
Movie Screening in Huxley 213 (Clore Lecture Theatre) @ 18:30

This week we are watching Paprika by legendary director Satoshi Kon. Paprika was the last feature length film he directed before his passing in 2010.

The world of dreams can be an incredible window into the psyche, showing one's deepest desires, aspirations, and repressed memories. The "DC Mini," is a device with the power to delve into the dreams of others. Atsuko Chiba and Kosaku Tokita have been tirelessly working to develop this technology with the hopes of using it to deeply explore patients' minds and help cure them of their psychological disorders.

However, when this technology is stolen and people around them start acting strangely, Atsuko and Kosaku know they have a serious problem on their hands, enlisting the help of Officer Konakawa to search both the real and dream worlds for their mental terrorist.

It's time for the first London Anime Union event of the year, the UCL Halloween Party!

6. ICon Pop Culture Convention

ICon is an Imperial College pop-culture convention planned, run by, and for students.

ICon runs on Saturday 21st October from 11:00 to 17:00, unfortunately clashing with the LAU Halloween party, so pick whichever sounds best. The event will be on the South Kensington Campus in CAGB Floor 6: Rooms 649-52 and 640 (the rooms are all next to each other and the first four are one long room).

Various ICL societies, such as Pokesoc, AnimeSoc, KpopSoc, KnitSoc, IC Cybersecurity, Gaming and Esports and several, independent student-sellers will each run a stall where you can learn more about what they do, participate in activities, and buy their products.

Imperial students do not have to purchase a ticket - just show up with your Uni ID and be prepared to give your email to sign-in.

For non-Imperial students, click here.

If you have any questions or need to know anything about us or our events, just email us at animesoc@imperial.ac.uk, or message us through Facebook or Discord!
